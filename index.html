The problem

Solana apps can execute fast. Control is still fragmented.

Solana programs can swap, lend, and settle in milliseconds. But the moment an action needs to touch another chain — bridging, wrapping, trusting a committee with your keys — speed doesn't matter if security is the bottleneck.

Bridges add trust and fragmentation

Every bridge introduces relayers, attestors, and wrapped assets that fracture liquidity and add attack surface.

Custody stacks break sovereignty

Enterprise custody solutions impose vendor lock-in, centralized key control, and opaque policy engines.

AI agents need rules, not root access

Agents get full keys or nothing. There's no way to scope authority, enforce limits, or require human approval when it matters.

The Ika approach

Ika turns signing into a programmable primitive.

Define who can act, on what, under which rules, and when. Keep the user required where it matters. Automate where policy allows. Let Solana apps and agents act across chains without replacing sovereignty with convenience.

Design principles

Zero-trust. Usable. Decentralized.

Most systems give you two. Ika is built for all three.

Z

Zero-trust

No single party — not even Ika — can reconstruct your private key. 2PC-MPC guarantees mathematical non-collusion, not just governance promises.

U

Usable

Sub-second signing. Programmable policies in Rust. Seamless integration with existing Solana programs. Security that doesn't sacrifice developer experience.

D

Decentralized

2,000+ validator nodes signing in parallel. Not the 4-8 node ceiling of legacy MPC networks. Real decentralization at scale.
