Architecture

The key never becomes a liability.

Ika uses 2PC-MPC so signing is computed jointly without reconstructing the private key in one place. That matters because most systems fail at the control point. They may decentralize governance language, but key control still collapses back to a small group of signers. Ika changes the control model itself.

Cross-chain demand is real.
The control layer is still broken.

Teams want to trade, lend, custody, automate, and settle across chains as if the market were one surface. Instead they get bridges, wrapped assets, relayers, and signer committees. That stack adds latency, operational drag, and the kind of trust assumptions that keep blowing up under size.

The Ika approach

Do not move the asset. Control it where it already sits.

Ika is a signing network for native cross-chain execution. Your application defines policy in Move. Ika produces the signature on the destination chain. No synthetic asset. No handoff to a bridge operator. No committee holding the full key because there is no full key sitting in one place to begin with.
