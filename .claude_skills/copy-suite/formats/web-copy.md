# Web Copy

## Workflow

1. Identify the page type (landing, product, about, pricing, hero)
2. Name the one job of this page in 5 words or fewer
3. Identify the target reader and their awareness level (unaware, problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware, most-aware)
4. Choose the framework that fits the awareness level
5. Draft using the section template for that page type
6. Run the output quality gate (see SKILL.md Quality Gate section)

## Framework Selection

| Awareness Level | Best Framework | Why |
|----------------|---------------|-----|
| Unaware | StoryBrand | Lead with their world, not your product |
| Problem-aware | PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve) | Name the pain, twist it, then resolve |
| Solution-aware | BAB (Before-After-Bridge) | Show the transformation your product enables |
| Product-aware | AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action) | They know you exist, build desire |
| Most-aware | Direct offer | They're ready. Don't oversell. State the offer. |

## Page Anatomy Quick Reference

### Landing Page
1. Hero (headline + subhead + CTA + social proof hint)
2. Problem section (name what's broken)
3. Solution section (how you fix it)
4. Benefits (3-5, outcome-focused not feature-focused)
5. Social proof (testimonials, logos, numbers)
6. How it works (3 steps, remove complexity)
7. FAQ / objection handling
8. Final CTA (restate the value, single action)

### Product Page
1. Product name + one-line value prop
2. Key visual context (what the reader sees)
3. Feature-benefit pairs (feature is what it does, benefit is why they care)
4. Social proof specific to this product
5. Pricing with anchor
6. CTA

### Pricing Page
1. Lead with value, not numbers
2. Plan comparison (anchor the expensive plan first)
3. Feature matrix (checkmarks, keep scannable)
4. FAQ targeting purchase objections
5. Money-back guarantee or risk reversal
6. CTA per plan

### About Page
1. Origin story (human, specific, vulnerable)
2. Mission (what you're building and why it matters)
3. Team (real humans, not stock bios)
4. Values (show, don't list)
5. CTA (what should they do after feeling connected?)

## Headline Principles

- Specificity beats cleverness ("Save 3 hours every week" > "Work smarter")
- Benefits beat features ("Wake up to a clean inbox" > "AI-powered email sorting")
- Numbers add credibility ("Join 12,847 founders" > "Join thousands")
- Questions work when the reader's answer is "yes" or "tell me more"
- Test: cover the headline. Can you still understand the page? If yes, the headline isn't doing enough.

## CTA Craft

- First person works: "Start my free trial" > "Start your free trial"
- Action + outcome: "Get the playbook" > "Download"
- One CTA per section. One primary CTA per page.
- Surround CTAs with micro-proof (testimonial, stat, guarantee)
- Never: "Submit," "Click here," "Learn more" (these are lazy)
