# Social Copy (Short-Form)

## Workflow

1. Identify the platform (X, LinkedIn, Instagram)
2. Name the one job of this post (make them feel, think, act, share?)
3. Choose the hook type
4. Draft in the platform's native voice
5. Cut ruthlessly (if you can say it in fewer words, do)
6. Run the output quality gate (see SKILL.md Quality Gate section)

## Hook Types

| Type | Pattern | Best For |
|------|---------|----------|
| Contrarian | "Most people think X. They're wrong." | Engagement (replies) |
| Curiosity gap | "I discovered something about X that changes everything." | Clicks, bookmarks |
| Bold claim | "X is the single biggest mistake you're making." | Authority building |
| Story opener | "Last Tuesday, something happened that..." | Emotional connection |
| Number-driven | "I analyzed 500 X. Here's what I found." | Bookmarks, credibility |
| Question | "Why does nobody talk about X?" | Replies, discussion |
| "Most people" | "Most people will never X. Here's why." | Identity signaling |

**Critical:** The hook earns the read. Everything after the hook earns the action. If the hook doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters.

## Engagement Psychology

Different actions require different psychological triggers:

| Action | Trigger | Write Copy That... |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| **Reply** | Opinion, emotion, identity | Makes them feel something they need to express |
| **Like** | Agreement, acknowledgment | Says what they already believe, clearly |
| **Retweet** | Identity signaling | Makes them look smart/interesting for sharing |
| **Bookmark** | Utility, future value | Teaches something they'll need later |
| **Follow** | Consistent value promise | Shows this account is worth coming back to |

## Platform Voice

| Platform | Tone | What Works | What Dies |
|----------|------|------------|-----------|
| X/Twitter | Sharp, authentic, opinionated | Hot takes, personal stories, useful threads | Corporate speak, hashtag spam, link-only posts |
| LinkedIn | Professional vulnerability, career lessons | "Here's what I learned" + lesson | Humblebrags, engagement bait, emoji walls |
| Instagram | Visual-first, caption supports image | Personal narrative, behind-the-scenes | Long paragraphs with no visual hook |

## Viral Patterns (Reverse-Engineered)

1. **"I did X. Here's what happened."** - Personal experiment + surprising result
2. **Contrarian list** - "5 things everyone does that actually don't work"
3. **Before/after** - Transformation with specific details
4. **Observation** - Name something everyone notices but nobody says
5. **"Nobody tells you"** - Insider knowledge framing
6. **Framework drop** - Give people a mental model they can use immediately

## Character Optimization (X/Twitter)

- **One-liner** (< 100 chars): Maximum impact. Reads clean in timeline. Good for quotable takes.
- **Two-punch** (100-200 chars): Setup + payoff. The tweet has a turn.
- **Full tweet** (200-280 chars): Room for a complete thought with context. Don't waste it on filler.
- **White space matters.** Line breaks create rhythm. A tweet with line breaks reads differently than a wall of text.

## What to Cut

- "I think" (you're posting it, they know you think it)
- "Just" (weakens everything it touches)
- "Very," "really" (find a stronger word)
- "In order to" (replace with "to")
- Hashtags in X copy (unless genuinely relevant)
- Throat-clearing first sentences ("So I've been thinking about...")
